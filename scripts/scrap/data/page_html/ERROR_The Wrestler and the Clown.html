Review by sakana1 ★★★★ Liked 4

What an absolute charmer The Wrestler and the Clown is! Tracing the careers of the titular circus performers, the film follows them from show to show, together and apart, from city to county and back again, and witnesses their kindness, loss, and resilience, closing with the happy ending for which the audience has been fervently hoping since the characters first came on screen.

I've seen this film compared to Raoul Walsh's wonderful Gentleman Jim, but I'd argue the two are in fact quite different, despite both being built around real-life champions in their fields. Whereas one of the great assets of Walsh's film is the way Jim Corbett (Errol Flynn) glides through life, encountering nary an obstacle on his way… more

Review by Diogo Serafim ★★★★½ Liked

The feelings on the stage and those of life behind the curtains. Arguably Barnet's most humane film, departing from Yudin's childhood memories and opposing them to the oppression of the ruling political and economic forces of the tsarism. It is a work marveled by the beauty of the world, while not being blind to its pain. The way each actor experiences grief, happiness and emotions in general in this film touches a very essential facet of cinema. The colors are also absolutely marvelous, and I love the pigs.

Review by Rishi Kowalski

No subtitles at the screening, but a really wonderful way to engage with this film still as a non russian speaker - much of it translated well and truly made me appreciate the acting performances, especially Chekan's. Barnet really complements his framing of actors, scenes, and places with the camera's movement. Cool stuff, hoping to see more soon!
