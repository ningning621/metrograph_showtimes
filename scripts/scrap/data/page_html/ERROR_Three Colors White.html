Three Colours: White

1994

Trois couleurs : Blanc

Directed by Krzysztof Kieślowski

Synopsis

Polish immigrant Karol Karol finds himself out of a marriage, a job and a country when his French wife, Dominique, divorces him after six months due to his impotence. Forced to leave France after losing the business they jointly owned, Karol enlists fellow Polish expatriate Mikołaj to smuggle him back to their homeland.

The worst in the trilogy is still...pretty good. The ending and theme are probably my favorite parts. I will say, at times the score does feel extremely “generic 90s drama” though. I guess what separates this from the others is just how normal it feels. While Blue and Red emphasize their respective colors and use them to create the tone of the film, this one just feels like a movie where the color white happens to be a theme. In it’s defense, white is harder to emphasize than something like blue or red, but it’s role in the film just felt like an afterthought rather than the main point like the others in the trilogy. But again, when looking at it on it’s own and not as a part of the group, it was a good time.
