Muna Moto

1975

Directed by Jean-Pierre Dikongué-Pipa

Synopsis

Ngando and Ndomé share an extremely perfect love. Yet, tradition demands a dowry for Ndomé’s hand that Ngando, an orphan, cannot afford. Forced to ask his uncle for assistance, Ngando finds himself at the mercy of his uncle’s lust and greed. How can Ngando and Ndomé find peace together? Their journey will be challenging and everyone will learn that not everything special can be saved.

Review by sakana1 2

Quite spectacular. Gorgeous to look at and political while also working on the level of simple, human emotions: a love story, crushed upon the twin rocks of the state and tradition, in which the patriarchy and covetousness reign supreme.

Women as the perpetual lesser, supporting one another when they are out of sight (reach) of the patriarchy, but so beholden to its will (its fists) that, when forced into a choice, they must turn their back on their sisters.

About the pain of having to bow to the will one's superiors (those who are richer, and older, and more male), and the absolute joy that still resides in every tiny moment of honest togetherness and affection. The physical connection can be severed by force, but the emotional one lives on.

… One can't stand unfairness throughout one's life. We have always obeyed. They should listen to us too.


Aboubakar Sanogo's essay for Criterion is excellent.
