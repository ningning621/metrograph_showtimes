4-Star Review: Fantasia (1940)
Walt Disney’s Fantasia (1940) represents one of the most audacious and artistically ambitious projects in the history of animation, a bold experiment that seeks to merge classical music with visual storytelling in a manner unprecedented for its time.

The film consists of eight distinct segments, each set to a different piece of classical music, ranging from Bach’s Toccata and Fugue in D Minor to Stravinsky’s The Rite of Spring. This anthology structure allows for remarkable variety in tone, style, and subject matter: abstract visualizations of musical form give way to narrative sequences (such as The Sorcerer’s Apprentice and The Pastoral Symphony), mythological spectacle (Night on Bald Mountain), and evolutionary drama (The Rite of Spring).
