Allegro non troppo

1976

Directed by Bruno Bozzetto

Synopsis

An enterprising producer believes he has hit upon a winning concept: a program of original animated shorts set to classical music. Undeterred by warnings that this has already been done by an American named ‘Prisney,’ he rallies an orchestra of geriatric women, a bullish conductor, and an animator that he keeps locked in the dungeon. What could go wrong?

Review by Conor Murnane

A hysterically funny and unashamedly cynical experience, one that savagely tackles such topics as American capitalism, war, sexual frustration, man's relationship with nature, clueless film producers and, overall, highbrow stuffiness and snobbery, all while presenting us with increasingly wild, surreal visuals, ranging from grand-scale and epic to simple and cartoony. The ultimate example of a spoof actually being just as good as, if not superior to, it's original source.

Review by Doc Lyon 2

1st Bruno Bozzetto

Italy does Fantasia, with appropriately cartoonish results. Fantasia is animation, a symphony of colour and line in graceful detailed motion. Allegro Non Troppo is a cartoon, in the tradition of newspaper strips; simple drawings, satirical topics. The title is both a musical term and, more generally, a warning about looking before you leap, an addendum to this film's caution about modernity and consumerism; at least two of the sequences end with the crushing presence of 20th century society destroying what came before.
